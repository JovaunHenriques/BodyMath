Neck Extension

  1. Slowly pull your head back and tuck your chin. This places your head directly above your shoulders.
  2. Slowly tilt your head back looking up at the ceiling.
  3. Gently rotate your head back and forth about three or four times. Your head should only turn about 1 centimeter. This helps to bring your neck all the way to end range.
  4. Stop when a stretch is felt in the muscles in the front of your neck.
  5. Hold for a count of 10.
  6. Repeat 5 times.

Dumbbell Reverse Lunge to Stepup

Why It Works: It strengthens the quads, along with the glutes and hamstrings, while also stabilizing the hips.

How to Do It:

  1. Hold dumbbells in both hands (25-35lbs/50-70lbs total).
  2. Stand facing a bench. Step back into a reverse lunge.
  3. As you come up from the lunge, step onto the bench with the same working leg.
  4. Step down, then lunge back with the opposite leg, coming back up, and immediately transitioning into a step up.
    5. Prescription: Reps: 10 on each side Sets: 3 Rest: 2 min. rest between sets
